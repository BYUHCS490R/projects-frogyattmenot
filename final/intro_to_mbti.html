The MBTI Concept

The framework of MBTI consists of eight preferences organized into four pairs of opposites. Our MBTI types is heavily influenced by our natural preferences in the four important of aspects of personality. (Reference Used: https://www.myersbriggs.org/my-mbti-personality-type/the-mbti-preferences/)

Preference Pairs
Dichotomy Description Preferences
Extraversion (E) or Introversion (I) These first preference pairs refers to how an individual direct/receive their energy and where they focus their attention on. Extraversion (E): Individuals who feel more energized by their interaction with their external world, consisting of people and things.
Introversion (I): Individuals who feel more energized by their internal world, consisting of thoughts, feelings, and reflections.
Sensing (S) or Intuition (N) These second preference pairs represents how an individual takes in information. Sensing (S): Individuals who pay more attention to information that is concrete; focuses on the what is, paying attention to existing facts; relies on their perception through their five senses(trusted experience).
Intuition (N): Individuals who pay more attention to the patterns and possibilities to the information they receive; focuses on the big picture and what if, making connections between facts; uses their five senses as well but rely on perception through insights and hunches (trust inspiration).
Thinking (T) or Feeling (F) These third preference pairs shows how an individual makes decisions and how they come to conclusion based on their judging mental processes. Thinking (T): Individuals who makes decisions based on objective facts; focuses mostly on logic and analysis.
Feeling (F): Individuals who makes decisions subjectively; more focused on the people involved, values and relationships.
Judging (J) or Perceiving (P) These fourth and last preference pairs describes how an individual deals with the outer world and how they approach their life. Judging (J): Individuals who prefers planned and organized approach to life; likes to have things settled and decided; prefers structured and firm decisions.
Perceiving (P): Individuals who prefer a flexible and spontaneous approach to life; likes to keep their options open; prefers to adapt and go with the flow.

Each preferences have their own strengths and weaknesses. Knowing our MBTI based on these preferences allows us to understand ourselves and others way better, providing opportunities for person growth and development. These gives a deeper insight of our personality.
