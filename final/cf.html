What are Cognitive Functions?

The cognitive functions are the building block that makes up the 16 personality type. They represent 8 distinct mental processes that used for making decisions an processing information. Each 16 personality types has their own unique order of these functions that are arranged based on priority, which is: the dominant, auxiliary, tertiary, and inferior function. The 8 Cognitive Functions

Introverted Intuition (Ni)

The types that employ Ni as either their dominant or auxiliary function are the intuitive (N) judging (J) types. Some traits of this function includes:

  • Relies more on sudden epiphany or on timely 'aha' moments when tackling unconventional tasks
  • Open-minded
  • Always finding ways to improve
  • Seeks synergy or collaboration
  • Knows how to use pattern recognition to narrow down information to one common aspect

Extraverted Intuition (Ne)

The types that possess Ne as their dominant or auxiliary function are the Intuitive (N) perceiving (P) types. Some traits of this function includes:

  • Makes connections; recognize ideas and relationships
  • Good at noticing and exploring potential possibilities
  • Good at guessing/predicting/theorizing
  • Skilled at pattern recognition to extrapolate information to come up with new, common aspects matching the pattern

Introverted Sensing (Si)

The types that utilize Si as their dominant or auxiliary function are the Sensing (S) judging (J) types. Some traits of this function includes:

  • Strong memory of what's already familiar and details
  • Practical
  • Specializing helps them with tasks and filling roles
  • Enjoys routine
  • Relies on the tried and true methods

Extraverted Sensing (Se)

The types that have Se as their dominant or auxiliary function are the Sensing (S) perceivers (P). Some traits of this function includes:

  • Quick and smooth response when handling whatever comes up in the moment
  • Uses their five senses and gut feeling
  • Enjoys new sensations
  • Observant of their surroundings
  • Lives in the present moment
  • Good memory for relevant details
  • In touch with body sensations

Introverted Thinking (Ti)

The types that use Ti as their dominant or auxiliary function are the Thinking (T) perceiving (P) types. Some traits of this function includes:

  • Deep thinkers
  • Has deductive thinking
  • Loves analysing a situation from every different angles and forming theories, framework or model
  • Relies on complex or abstract logic

Extraverted Thinking (Te)

The types that possess Te as their dominant or auxiliary function are the Thinking (T) judging (J) types. Some traits of this function includes:

  • Values time and higly efficient
  • Believes that facts speak for themselves
  • makes decisions objectively
  • Good at organizing people and resources to achieve goals
  • Focuses on productivity and results

Introverted Feeling (Fi)

The types that utilize Fi as their dominant or auxiliary function are the Feeling (F) perceiving (P) types. Some traits of this function includes:

  • Heavily guided by their personal values and morals
  • Highly empathetic; good at understanding others' emotions and perspective
  • Values ansd appreciates authenticity and staying true to oneself

Extraverted Feeling (Fe)

The types that employ Fe as their dominant or auxiliary function are the Feeling (F) judging (J) types. Some traits of this function includes:

  • Evaluates and communicates values
  • Build trust and enhance relationships
  • Attentive and curious
  • Patient and good listeners
  • Skilled at reading social cues and adapting to social situations
  • Uses emotions as compass to navigate themselves through social and emotional situations
